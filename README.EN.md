# The ETmbit Project

The ETmbit project provides a **complete robotics learning pathway** and makes teaching robotics simple and accessible. The project is aimed at:

* upper primary education
* lower secondary education

ETmbit uses the **micro:bit + MakeCode** platform. Robot components are either purchased **ready-made** or **3D printed**. Each lesson includes a **building guide** (including parts lists and 3D printer files), a **teacher’s manual**, and of course the **lesson materials**. Only **minimal prior knowledge and preparation** are expected from teachers and instructors.

<br>

## :twisted_rightwards_arrows: Lesson Plan

ETmbit is based on the principle of **differentiated learning**. Students choose their own route through the lesson materials based on interest, level, and learning style. Teachers use a **tracking system** to guide their progress. No complicated slide decks — everything works with a single, clear **roadmap** that provides an overview for both students and teachers.

:large_orange_diamond: [roadmap](.method/EN/et-en-roadmap.pdf)

<br>

## :basket: Lesson Content

The key idea here is **learning through play**. It starts with several familiar games to learn how MakeCode works: *cat-and-mouse*, *snake*, *simon says*. The lesson materials include many **challenges**. Through different routes within the ETmbit material, students ultimately develop the same skills. There is something for everyone:

:soccer: sports, :musical_note: music, :cartwheeling: dance, :video_game: games, :seedling: nature, :baby: care, :nut_and_bolt: technology, :clapper: media, etc.
